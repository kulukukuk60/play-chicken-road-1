    One round from take-off to vanish

    Every round follows the same pattern. Bets open for a short window, the road chicken takes off, and the multiplier starts climbing from 1.00x. At a random point fixed before the round begins, the run ends and the chicken disappears from the road. If you cashed out before that moment, your stake is paid according to the multiplier shown; if you stayed too long, the stake is lost.

    Because the result is locked in before the round starts, no late tap can change it. Past rounds do not shape the next one either, whether you call it chicken road, chickenroad, or road chicken game. The only real choice is when to leave the round.

    Why players can audit results

    The better versions of chickenroad use a provably fair system. Before the round starts, the server commits to a secret seed and publishes its hash. The final result is then built from that seed and values tied to the round, so the outcome cannot be changed after bets are placed.

    The hash shown before take-off locks the round in place. That means neither the operator nor any player can alter the departure point once betting is open, and the result can be checked later.

    After the round ends, the seed is revealed and a verifier can compare it with the published hash. For Philippine players, licensed sites are the safer path because clones do not always include the same fairness tools.
